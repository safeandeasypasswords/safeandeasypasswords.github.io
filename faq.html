What is a password schema?
Password schema is a mental algorithm that you can use to generate your passwords on the fly i.e., you don't need to memorize passwords anymore! You can simply run these algorithms in your head with no need of paper, pencil or any computing device. Click on Three Word Schema to see an example of such a schema.
Why should I use a password schema?
Security of online information is summarized in passwords. The number of online accounts that we use is increasing rapidly and memorizing different passwords is not feasible anymore. To avoid this difficulty, people either share the same password between multiple accounts or they use password manager apps. It has been seen that both of these methods are vulnerable to security threats. As a solution, we propose use of simple mental algorithms which we call password schemas. Our algorithms have proven security guarantees.
Do I need to memorize passwords for different websites?
No! Everytime that you want to login to a website, you will generate/regenerate your password.
What if I don't use a website for a few months? Will I still remember the password?
You don't have to remember your password, you can easily regenerate it in your head.
If I want to adopt a password schema, I should change all of my current passwords, this is a lot of effort! Why should I do this?
We agree that the initial effort to adopt our password schemas might seem high. But, once you adopt it for your old accounts, we promise that it will make your life much easier because of the following reasons:
  • Your passwords look like a random combination of letters/numbers and therefore it's very hard to crack.
  • You don't need to remember any of your passwords
  • You don't even need to know if you have an account with a website! Generate your password with your algorithm, if it works, you have an account, otherwise create an account with the same password that you just generated.
  • If you want to make a new account, you don't need to design a new password. Just give the name of the website/app as input to your algorithm and generate a password.
  • No "forgot my password" anymore!
These schemas are public, what does guarantee that my passwords are secure?
Although the schemas are public, your key is privately stored in your head. Remember that this is the only private information that you should remember. We prove that, if you chose your key randomly (not based on your personal information), your passwords are hard to crack.
How difficult is it for an adversary to hack into my accounts?
It depends on the schema that you use. For example, if you use the Three Word Schema , the adversary need to steal approximately seven of your passwords to be able to guess your password for a new website.
